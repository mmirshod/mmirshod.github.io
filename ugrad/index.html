Essay 1: The Global UGRAD Program is for young leaders committed to serving their home communities. Why would you be a great participant in the Global UGRAD Program?
Essay 1:
Growing up in Uzbekistan made my journey into life much more interesting and complicated simultaneously. Since my primary school years, I have begun to seek ways to become a better version of myself and invent something useful for the community. But no man is an island; that is why I began creating teams to achieve higher goals. And now, The Global UGRAD Programme is offering once-in-a-lilifetime chance with which I can expand my worldview, get fresh ideas, and make more connections worldwide.

Ultimately, my ambitions are much higher than just improving myself; I also try to seize global issues like low cyberliteracy and a high rate of corruption by promoting IT studies and applying digitalization to increase the level of transparency in government agencies, at least in my region. These problems correspond to United Nations Sustainable Development Goals 4 and 16, Quality Education and Peace, Justice, and Strong Institutions, respectively.

The Global UGRAD Program provides an outstanding opportunity to bridge my local aspirations with global challenges. Participation in this programme will serve as a springboard for me to improve my leadership abilities, gain fresh viewpoints, and make new friends worldwide. So this programme is not just a step forward in my study or career path, but an opportunity to channel the knowledge and experience of American professors into developing Uzbekistan on the world stage.

As an example of my commitment to local people, my recent participation in the Mapathon—Mapping for Water with initiative from "Uzsuvtaminot". This event was designed to survey people in Yangiyul District (Tashkent Region) about the water resources and map each piece of data to GIS software. I played a crucial role as the main GIS analyst of our team, who coordinated all actions of the group throughout the mapathon. In addition, analysts had to analyse and visualise the data through interactive web maps and charts. Due to the mapathon, I have not only improved my technical and leadership skills, but it has also made me appreciate what I have because many people do not have access to basic resources like drinking water.

Alongside my commitment to people, I have a strong academic foundation. I am a sophomore student majoring in Information and Communication Engineering. Simultaneously, I am self-studying Programming through YouTube and other resources and have already obtained an Applied Software Engineering Fundamentals Specialisation from IBM. Also, as a freshman, I have been awarded a 50% scholarship, a testament to my outstanding academic performance. In addition, I am showcasing my leadership skills even in university by being president of Academic Support and Volleyball clubs.

The key component that perfectly synchronises with my career and personal objectives is that the Global UGRAD Programme offers leadership development workshops and seminars, which will provide me with the tools and knowledge to become a more effective leader. Additionally, the programme's cultural exchange component includes activities such as language immersion and interaction with local communities, which will broaden my understanding of different cultures and perspectives. Lastly, the programme's commitment to community engagement can provide me with opportunities to apply my technical skills and knowledge to address local challenges, such as through volunteer projects or internships.

To sum up, my experience, ambitions, knowledge, and skills that I gained from my local university and teachers, in line with my commitment to the community, make me an ideal candidate for the Global UGRAD Programme. As a young leader from a developing country, I do not want to wait for a change that will improve our country, but to be that change and stay in everyone's memory.

Essay 2:
Growing up in one of the developing countries, Uzbekistan, I became a witness to the powerful impact of visionary leaders—someone who challenges conventions and inspires change—on the development of the community itself. Among the numerous exceptional people, one person stands out greatly from all others: the rector of Inha University in Tashkent, Muzaffar Djalalov. Evidence of this may be that, according to the “Yuksalish” movement, Muzaffar Djalalov is included in the TOP-35 list of young leaders in the country.

Muzaffar Djalalov was born on February 17, 1986, in Tashkent. He is the embodiment of both academic and leadership excellence, as well as an example of commitment to his homeland. His journey from a regular researcher at the Center for Scientific, Technical, and Marketing Research of the State Unitary Enterprise "UNICON.UZ" to the youngest rector in the history of all of Uzbekistan is fascinating! In addition to these personality traits, his openness to everything new, as well as the positive energy and charisma that emanated from him, made him my role model.

Muzaffar Aka is not different because of his great education, graduating from Tashkent University of Information Technologies and getting a PhD from a top-ranked university in Korea when he was 30. But what really makes him stand out is that he chose to go back to Uzbekistan instead of continuing his successful career in the technology field in South Korea after 3 years of studying and working there. He started as a regular researcher and worked on big projects. Now he is the rector of a leading IT university in Uzbekistan. All of these show his willingness to be part of the development of ICT in Uzbekistan.

Muzaffar Aka is fluent in four languages, and he is the best negotiator when it comes to working with big companies from around the world like Kaspersky Lab, USAID, and Coursera. This has made me see what effective leadership looks like. I really like the way he leads with kindness and positivity; that means a lot to me. As a student, seeing how much he supports projects and startups, including the successful "Tahrirchi" app, has influenced my understanding of what being a leader really means. His commitment to helping students' projects and new start-ups, along with his recent involvement in the Young Leaders program with the UAE government, inspires me to see a future where my actions have a major impact on people in my homeland, Uzbekistan, just like him.

He is also well-known for his great interpersonal ties as well as his outstanding achievements as a professional specialist. His closeness with professors, administrative staff, and students makes you look at him not only as your boss but also as your friend. A friend who always tries to help you in all situations. Proof of this can be seen in recent victories at multiple hackathons and other technical events.

In conclusion, Muzaffar Djalalov's journey as a leader shows that he cares about supporting his students, learning new things, and collaborating with global juggernauts. He has had a big influence on my academic excellence at university, the way I am leading my teammates, and my passion for the development of Uzbekistan as an IT giant. As time passes, seeing how successful he is and how he helps his community keeps inspiring me in my own life. Muzaffar Aka is more than just a leader; he sets a great example for young people in Uzbekistan.
